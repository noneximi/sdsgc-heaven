Farming

  • For Fast Fort Solgres farming, run Howzer, BGuila, & RGowther, sub Green Merlin on red evolution books. Guila gets an ulti orb if she isn't damaged, and Gowther gives the team an atk buff every turn he's not damaged for a limit of 5 turns. Clearing each wave in one turn activates all these uniques. This team has really good synergy and the fastest clear times depending on your own setup and gear.
  • Howzer can solo most content if he's properly invested in. Use him solo to farm easy stages. He has AOE-only Skill Set.
  • Choose from these to build your farm team: GLizhawk, Weindhart, BGuila, Gowther and Twiggo. Check their skills, passives and synergy with each other, then decide. Howzer should always be on your farm team if possible. For sub slot you either want to have a unit to level up passively or units like red jericho and green merlin for fast clears.

Where to Farm Gold

  • The fastest and most reliable way to get a lot of gold is auto farming red books 24/7 on Fort Solgres during 50%OFF Stamina, and is why you need a fast and reliable auto farming team, for these discount stamina events.
  • RAIDS! If you only do extreme raids, and have fast clearing times, on non stamina off days you can focus on spamming some. As drops you can get a bunch of SSR Chests, so if you can, complete a few chapters' boss battles each day so you can get chalices, gold, SSR chests, awakening mats/stones, & SR gear. To maximize your gold gains, always eat gold food in boss battles and raids.
  • Fort Solgres Gold dungeons, always do the gold dungeons, they are limited. After clearing them, invite Veronica and get a 20% gold increase per sold SSR gold chest. Boost applies to all items you can sell at town shops.
  • Hawk's sales, level the 4k foods to lvl 5, and fill your 5 spots with them.

What Food to Farm

  • PVP Coins Food
  • Gold Food
  • Hawk's Sales food till lvl 5
  • Affinity food, for your main team
  • For PVP high ranks. Evasion, Ulti+1, Atk, Hp food is popular decide which one is best for your team.

Where to Farm Anvils & Awakening Stones

  • Salvaging your gear nets you a really neat amount of Awakening stones. +1 Gives more Red Stones, +5 Gives more Blue Stones, so +5 your SSR Gear and +1 the rest before salvaging for best efficiency as well as time.
  • Raids, you can get 5 or 4 awakening stones, and you can do them without stamina (partner raids).
  • Guild Boss for 50 weekly anvils.
  • PVP for 100 weekly anvils
  • Weapon Gacha for Anvils, awakening stones, and gear, but don't do this unless you can spare the gold. Wait for events (like the Salvaging Event) unless you are in a rush, although it's not a bad place to spend your gold.

What Specialized Gear Sets to Build

  • 1 CDMG Set, Bracer and Ring ideally. Either ATK or CDMG sub stats, but be sure that these stats are over 80%. Alternatively you can build a CDMG/CDMG/CDMG gear set.
  • 1 or 2 DEF/DEF/DEF sets. Same as above you can go with either Defense or Resistance sub stats. When you're done with that, you can worry about min maxing.
  • 1 or 2 sets with Pierce sub stats. There's more variety here, it can work with ATK/DEF or ATK/CDMG for Howzer, HP/DEF sets for Gustav or if you want a CC Stick on Howzer.
  • Positioning of your gear matters. For instance, Ring and Bracer is ideal on CDMG DPS because then it would be much simpler to exchange and it's already built the way you want the Crit DPS to work. It's hard to explain, but it does matter. It varies from person to person based on gear you have availabe, so adapt yourself, and proceed with what you have.
