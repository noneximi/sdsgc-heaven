General Game Tips

Guides by ★Arakito★

Ephemeral#0781

Cosmetics

  • Don't buy cosmetics, farm them.
  • Max affinity of your main characters.
  • Get other character versions to UR or SSR to get their costumes.
  • Buy SR and SSR weapons in pop up shop that are bought for gold. Get 40% discount before buying, raids plus 30k donations in town, they cost 300k for SSR and 150k for SR with discount.
  • If you already got everything you could on the character via Evolve, Affinity and Pop-UP shop, consider getting some UR cosmetics for those characters that don't have many cosmetics.

PVP

  • Always eat pvp coin food, unless you're climbing to Champion 1 in ungeared and need actual food, or you're trying to climb to Challenger in geared to secure some weekly pvp coins.
  • Stacking Passives work really good on pvp teams. Consider using guild boss Gowther gear in pvp, it works. Gowther gets a party buff every time he doesn't receive damage. Don't ignore the opponent Gowther either!
  • Buy Anvils first, weapons if you need them for ungeared or for units like Jericho, Green Merlin, or Guila to increase your efficiency in other content. Aim for 5/5 cosmetics, but anvils are the priority.
  • Consider getting SSR CDMG Gear for Bracer and Ring, then if you want a 3 CDMG SET, consider getting the rest.

Town Donations and Shops

  • Donate 30k to towns after clearing their raid, for the 40% shop discount.
  • Then proceed to buy Food Materials, which are cheap. I really recommend doing this in all towns, over time it's a better option than farming every food. Eventually you won't need to worry about farming food mats for affinity.
  • If you have enough gold, it's also worthwhile buying awakening materials, those that you have on low quantity.
  • Buying chalices is also another option, it runs up to 7Million for three 6 Chalices. It may be worth it if you have enough money, although it's not a priority.
  • 28 Guild Coins (Level 7 Guild) per day by just playing and donating is not half bad for your own progression, it's a win-win.

Character Ultimate Levels

  • For PVP this is extremly important in most cases, it can be the deciding factor in ungeared or geared. Good units to max are Blue King, Red Gowther, Green Merlin, Red Howzer, Red Arthur, Red Helbram, Blue Diane, Green Escanor, etc. For a variety of reasons, some work really good in ult rush comps, like Kingbram, Howzer; some are just really good like Green Merlin, she's useful anywhere, RGowther as well. Depending on how you build Gowther his ulti can one shot some teams. Arthur is an amazing link unit for Merlin and is also a really good CC Staple with a really strong ultimate, etc. The decision depends on what you run and what's your aim for your team.
  • SR Units are really easy to 6/6 and they will be your main association for a long time until you get other SSRs to 6/6, so consider investing on good SR associations for your main team.

Farming

  • For Fast Fort Solgres runs, do Howzer, Guilia, Gowther, Green Merlin on red evolution books, Guilia gets an ulti orb if she isn't damaged, and Gowther gets the team have a whole atk buff every turn he's not damage for a limit of 5 turns, being that you'll clear each wave in one turn (not hit at all) because of this, the team have really good synergy and the fastest clear times depending on your own setup and gear.
  • Howzer can solo most content if he's properly invested. Use him solo to farm easy stages. He have a whole AOE Skill Set.
  • To build your farm team, choose from these, Glizhawk, Weindhart, SR Guilia, Gowther and Twiggo, check their skills, passives and synergy with each other, then decide. Howzer should always be on farm team if possible, for sub slots you either want to have an unit to level or units like red jericho, or green merlin for fast clearings.

Positioning

  • Card draw is predetermined for the first turn so that you will always mirror your card draw.
  • If you get a slot 1 draw, then your ally will also get a slot 1 draw. If you get a slot 2 draw then your ally will also get a slot 2 draw and so on.
  • So to maximise the chances, it's best to stagger your DPS and Gowther in the first slots to increase the likelihood of getting good RNG.

Raid Exchange

  • So basically, you spawn 3 raids, while you partner also spawn 3 raids, then you two do these 6 raids at once. Without having to worry about changing equipment or about your partner composition. You can then do 3 more if you two want, or over the day. You can do 6 at once each if you wish, but talk it out with your partner first.
  • Is the fastest way and most efficient way to approach this that nets the most gains in short time without stressing you out, asking for raids every boss you spawn, or doing it with randoms and stressing over their performance. It can really burn you out.
  • After you finish the raids you can go clear the shops, if your partner did 3 different chapters than you, you can also clear all of them with 40% OFF for food mats, pop up shop weapon, awakening stones if you need them or chalices, etc.
  • So having or asking for a partner to exchange raids with is much more stress free than do them separately, you just need to coordinate once about the partner team, and then you can proceed with the same strategy the whole run. Making it much faster and tolerable while also increasing your overall farming speed and efficiency.
